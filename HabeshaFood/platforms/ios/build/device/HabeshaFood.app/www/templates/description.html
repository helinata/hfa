Habesha Food

People from Ethiopia and Eritrea are called Habesha. Habeshas typical meal consists of injera and several kinds of wot (stew). This cuisine consists of vegetable and often very spicy meat dishes. This is usually in the form of wot (sauce) served on top of injera, a large sourdough flatbread, which is about 50 centimeters (20 inches) in diameter and made out of fermented teff flour. Injera is used to scoop up a variety of spicy stews and salads found on any plate. Habesha people eat with their hands, using pieces of injera to pick up bites of entrées and side dishes.

Some terms explained:

Berbere is a spice mixture whose constituent elements usually include chili peppers, garlic, ginger, basil, black pepper (korarima), rue, Carom seeds , Fennel flower (coriander seeds), and fenugreek. It is a key ingredient in the cuisine. You can find this item from your local Ethiopian grocery store.

Mitmita is another powdered spicy food. It is more spicy than berbere and usually served as a side dish. You can find this item from your local Ethiopian grocery store.

Shiro powder is made from peas and chickpeas that are dried and ground into a fine powder, shiro powder. The flavor is rich, spiced and nutty, and this powder comes blended with different spices and herbs – fenugreek, cardamom, and dried basil to name a few – as well as dried garlic.

Besso powder is a roasted barley that is ground into fine powder.

Tej a honey wine that is brewed and consumed mostly in Ethiopia and Eritrea. It is flavored with the powdered leaves and twigs of gesho (Rhamnus prinoides).
